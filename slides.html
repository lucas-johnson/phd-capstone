The Paris Climate Agreement

  • Limit “the increase in global average temperature to well below 2° C above pre-industrial levels”
    • Dangerous and cascading effects beyond this threshold
  • “to limit the temperature increase to 1.5° C above pre-industrial levels”
    • To avoid the most drastic and devastating impacts

IPCC reporting

  • “More likely than not” that we will reach the 1.5° C threshold by 2040
  • Very low emissions scenario: 1.4° C
  • Intermediate emission scenario: 2.7° C
  • High emission scenario: 4.4° C
  • Most successful pathways rely on CO₂ removal technology (CDR)

IPCC - Climate Change 2023: Synthesis Report - https://doi.org/10.59327/ipcc/ar6-9789291691647.001

Natural climate solutions

  • Most mature and cost-effective CDR options

  • Potential to offset ~21% of US net annual emissions

  • Forests make up the largest proportion of these sequestration potentials

Stock-change methods and carbon accounting

  • Need repeated measurements / estimates / inventories

  • Differences between time 1 and time 2

  • Positive differences (growth) = removals from the atmosphere

  • Negative differences (disturbance) = emissions to the atmosphere

Aggregation to reduce uncertainty

  • Individual pixel predictions are highly variable

  • Pixels are not ecologically or practically meaningful

  • Aggregating smooths out variation

  • Pixel-level errors may cancel out

  • The degree to which they cancel out depends on spatial correlation
